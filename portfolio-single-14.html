Contacts & Pain Points

Careful Targets To Aim For

VP Logistics

C-Level that pays attention to transport and storage of items that would focus more toward fleet and yard management for docking purposes.

VP Transportation

C-Level role that wants to save money on a more efficient method while having full visibility of inbound/outbound shipments.

VP Supply Chain

C-Level's main focus is on saving/making money with their routes, suggest software that has Automation Optimizer to reduce Deadhead miles.

Logistics Manager

Prioritize on control on inbound/outbound shipments for all sites, in need of software with Advanced Scheduling and full Fleet Visibility.

Transportation Manager

Care about streamlining their operations with easier and better tools, especially if their work can become digital for their staff and drivers.

Chief Supply Chain Officer

Priority on streamlining their operation with multiple shippers and carriers, need a software can can automate tasks while reducing costs.

Case Study

Client: Aurora Organic Dairy

Date: 1th Februery 2020

Online: Aurora Research

With their outbound logistics and transfer movements having achieved steady state operations and users comfortably enjoying the benefits of increased operational efficiency, Aurora Organic Dairy is now looking forward to the next phase of its automation initiative. First up, AOD is significantly advancing its ability to increase logistics velocity deploying PCS’s powerful Advanced Scheduling Module for superior dock scheduling DC throughput. After that, AOD plans to deploy PCS Software to manage inbound logistics, bringing raw milk shipments into production facilities from farms/producers in disparate locations.
  • AOD’s business had grown to between 250-300 truckload shipments per week
  • “PCS implementation managers really set the table for the project with clear and well-conceived project management plans, best practices and protocols”
  • AOD plans to deploy PCS Software to manage inbound logistics, bringing raw milk shipments into production facilities from farms/producers in disparate locations
  • Critical factor pursuing new markets and maintaining competitive advantage

    Demo Questions

    Gather Qualifying Information

    Verify - First/Last Name, E-mail, Phone #
    1. How did you hear about us?
    2. What type of trucking do you primarily do?
      1. Full Truckload (FTL)
      2. Less-than-Truckload (LTL)
        1. Do you operate a warehouse(s) for freight storage?
        2. Are you cross docking freight?
      3. Intermodal
        1. Do you primarily operate in the rail, or port space?
        2. Are you primarily importing, or exporting freight?
        3. Do you own your own chassis?
      4. How many loads are you dispatching every month?
    3. Do you operate a freight brokerage?
      1. What services are you using for onboarding carriers?
      2. What services are you using for tracking carriers?
      3. How many loads are you brokering every month?
    4. Fleet Information
      1. How many trucks/trailers are in your fleet?
        • Brokerage Only: How many carriers are in your network?
      2. How many loads are you dispatching every month?
      3. What type of freight do you haul?
      4. What ELDs/GPS do you use? (Electronic-Logging-Device)
      5. What fuel card(s) do you use?
    5. Current Operations
      1. Are you currently using a TMS?
        • Yes: Why are you looking to switch (i.e., what are your pain points)?
        • No: How are you currently managing your operations?
      2. What are you using for accounting?
        • If they are using Quickbooks: Are you using QB Online or QB Desktop?
      3. How many office personnel do you have?
      4. Who are your primary customers (i.e., how do you receive loads?)
        • Brokers/Load Boards (DAT, Truckstop, etc.)
        • Shippers
      5. How are you receiving loads?
        • Email, Web Portal, EDI, API
    6. What are the key components you are looking for in a TMS?
    7. What other companies are you talking to?
    8. Are there any other stakeholders involved in this decision?
      • What are their roles?
      • What are their needs from the solution?
    9. What is your budget?
    10. What is your timing? When are you looking to implement a solution?
    Qualifying Prospect Questions (Build Rapport)
    Verify - First/Last Name, E-mail, Phone #
    1. Why are you looking at a TMS?
      1. Do you have an existing solution?
      2. If not, how are you managing your transportation network?
    2. What are the key components you are looking for in a solution?
      1. Load Planning / Optimization
        1. Where are you planning shipments?
        2. What tools are you using?
        3. Are you doing static routing?
      2. Routing / Tendering?
      3. Track & Trace?
      4. Exceptional Management?
      5. Freight Audit & Settlement
    3. How will you get orders/shipments into a TMS?
      1. Manual Entry?
      2. Flat File Uploads?
      3. Templates?
      4. Integration?
        1. What ERP (order management) system are you using?
        2. Will we be integrating with them?
    4. What other companies are you talking to? (They may or may not answer but ask the question)
    5. Who are the other stakeholders involved in the decision?
      1. What are their roles?
      2. What are their needs / wants from the solution?
      3. What solutions are they familiar with or have used in the past?
    6. What is the timing? When would you be looking to implement the solution?
    7. Is there a budget for this project?
      1. Is the budget based on what the system can do?
